Muḥammad al-Mishrī: The Love of Allah as the Ultimate End of All Aspirations All praise is due to Allah. My beloved ones—peace be upon you, and the mercy of Allah Most High and His blessings. Thereafter: I praise Allah for what I hear of you concerning His remembrance. For remembrance is a sign of freedom from heedlessness, and freedom from heedlessness is a sign of love, and love is a sign of Allah’s love. And whoever is loved by Allah has attained the ultimate end of all aspirations, as indicated by the saying: “When I love him, I am him.” So hold fast to remembrance (dhikr) and vigilant-awareness (muraqabah) for through these two the successful have truly succeeded. People differ in their standing with Allah according to the degree of their muraqabah, and the people of hearts are the people of Allah. Whoever wishes to be foremost and not overtaken must hasten toward muraqabah. Any time that passes without muraqabah—let one be certain that what was lost of goodness in that moment exceeds all that was gained throughout one’s entire past. Let him return to Allah and reclaim what has passed by saying, “Indeed we belong to Allah, and to Him we return.” Tears are too little in proportion to what has been lost of goodness in that time. Let him strive with the times that remain, so that no more is lost. Seek assistance through abundant prayers upon the Messenger of Allah ﷺ , through performing the prayer at its proper times with its conditions, in congregation, and through attending the wazīfah. Attendance is a condition for one who has access to a congregation; whoever performs it alone, it does not suffice him. There is no alternative to earnest striving in the matters of the religion. I ask Allah to grant success to me and to you, and to look upon us with the gaze of His pleasure and acceptance. — Muḥammad al-Mishrī Muhammad Al Mishri 4 groups يرزقنا الله تبارك وتعالى نتيجتها و The lifespans of those within the ummah of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ are short, from sixty to seventy years, and none of us knows how much he has left. So let us prepare for our return by constantly seeking forgiveness, praying upon the Prophet, and remembering Allah (t.w.t.). If people look down on us today and say that we are crazy because we remember Allah, and they mock us, nonetheless we will be mocking them on the Day of Resurrection when we will be “upon a seat of truth with a Powerful King,” while they will be in a place far from Allah (tw.t.), because they had forgotten Him in this world. Just as they forgot Him, Allah will deal with them in the Hereafter based on their forgetting Him in this world. Whoever is not among the people of Allah in this world, when the Hereafter comes, he will not be among the people of Allah. Whoever is not among the people of Paradise in this world will not be among the people of Paradise on the Day of Resurrection. We hope in Allah (t.w.t.) that we be among Allah’s beloveds, who are those who busy themselves with Him, He is satisfied with them, and they are satisfied with Him. We hope also that we will be among those whom Allah (t.w.t.) has made rich in this world and rich in the Hereafter. There are some people who are rich in this world and poor in the Hereafter; those are the infidels who have a lot of money. They are the kings in this world, but they are decisively the poor in the afterlife. There are some people who are poor in this world—the needy Muslims—but who will be the rich ones on the Day of Resurrection before Allah (t.w.t.). Then there are some people who are rich in this world and rich in the Hereafter: they are rich in this world because they earn their livelihood legally, they do not eat what is haram, and they spend in the way of Allah. Allah would always return their sustenance to them and spare them from needing others such that they would be kings in this world. Then, when the Day of Resurrection comes, they will also be kings. We ask Allah (t.w.t.) to make us among them suffice us from Allah’s (t.w.t.) creation, and suffice us with Allah (t.w.t.), in this world and in the Hereafter. Whoever among you wants the Tijani tariqah, I will tell him its conditions and tell him what the Tijani tariqah is, since many deny the Tijani tariqah and label its people infidels and sources of misguidance, all the while not knowing what the Tijani tariqah is—if you were to ask them about it they would tell you “I don’t know anything about it except that I heard the people just saying so and so.” The wird of the Tijani tariqah is istighfar a hundred times, Salatul-Fatihi a hundred times, and tahlil a hundred times. This is the Tijani wird, and all of this has been commanded by Allah (t.w.t.). He said, “Ask forgiveness from your Lord, for He is Oft-Forgiving.” He said, “Indeed, Allah and His angels send salah upon the Prophet; O you who believe, send salah and salam upon him,” and in the hadith: “Whoever makes one salah upon me, Allah makes ten salawat upon him. Whoever makes ten salawat upon me, Allah makes one hundred salawat upon him. Whoever makes one hundred salawat upon me, Allah makes one thousand salawat upon him. And whoever makes one thousand salawat upon me, his shoulder will compete with my shoulder at the gate of Paradise.” So this, what the Tijanis have, is the same as what is in the Qur’an and Hadith. They only have what is in the Qur’an and Hadith. In it is narrated in a hadith, “Remember Allah until they say that he’s crazy.” The Tijanis have nothing but seeking forgiveness, making salah upon the Prophet, and remembrance of Allah. Abdullahi wuld al-Hajj al-Alawi al-Tijani, RA Adab With the Sheikh Etiquette with the Sheikh as Seen by Sidi Muhammad al-Mishri After the Sheikh’s visit to Nigeria in 1971, during which he was accompanied by Sidi Muhammad al-Mishri, a scholarly exchange took place with the senior leaders of the Tijaniyya Fayda in the region. Sidi Muhammad al-Mishri offered beneficial and insightful guidance, which he later documented in writing. Here is the text of his notes: In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful. Praise be to Allah for granting me the favor of accompanying the honorable Sheikh to Lagos, the capital of Nigeria. May Allah protect it and its people, Amen. The leaders of the Tijaniyya and the people of the Fayda warmly welcomed the Sheikh, and discussions were held with the mentioned leaders, including Muhammad al-Thani Kafing, who, in truth, is the foremost Muhammad due to his precedence with the Sheikh—a great merit and distinction. Also present was Sayyid Malam Tahir, a noble and remarkable individual, embodying excellence for the era and standing out as a champion in every field. The discussion centered around the concept of etiquette with the Sheikh. I addressed the brothers, greeting them and expressing gratitude to Allah, as well as joy at this gathering. I then explained that true spiritual benefit from the Sheikh comes from heartfelt connection with his station. Let us focus our hearts on his spiritual rank, so we do not forget outward etiquette with his physical presence. If outward blessings depended solely on proximity, the people of Kaolack would be closer and dearer to the Sheikh than the people of Nigeria. The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, said regarding prayer, “Approach it with tranquility and composure.” Similarly, I say with full sincerity that the Sheikh is like prayer: “Approach him with tranquility and composure.” These teachings are not something the Sheikh would explicitly convey out of etiquette and modesty, just as it was the habit of the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him. However, the Quran revealed such teachings, as in Allah’s words: “Indeed, those who call you from behind the chambers—most of them do not use reason.” And His words: “Indeed, those who lower their voices in the presence of Allah’s Messenger, they are the ones whose hearts Allah has tested for righteousness.” These Quranic lessons continue to apply to the successors of the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him. It is clear that Allah manifests both beauty and majesty. When we witness Allah’s beauty manifesting in His servant, we must remember that His majesty is always watching over us. We must exercise utmost caution to avoid any lapse in etiquette out of reverence for Allah’s majesty. I shared with them that we, their Mauritanian brothers, were graced by the Sheikh’s visit to our land. Despite our love for him—if not equal to yours, then not much less—when he visited us, very few of us physically approached him or shook his hand. Some of our most respected leaders refrained from shaking his hand, instead greeting him with tear-filled eyes, hearts overflowing with joy, gratitude, and praise to Allah. While Allah’s divine beauty may urge us to merge spirit with spirit and body with body, His majesty compels us to hold back outwardly, which in truth is a progression forward spiritually. If you practice this approach with the Sheikh, you will gain more from his outward and inward presence. During the collective litany (wazifa) recited with the Sheikh, if we were able to remain completely still in reverence and modesty until he completed the session, prayed for us, and departed without anyone moving, that would be the ideal. Interacting with the Sheikh should be through hearts, not bodies. May Allah unite our hearts with his in outwardness, inwardness, and the innermost depths. I wish you all goodness and well-being. Peace be upon you. What is the Deen? “There is ultimately only one deen, i.e. following the Book and Sunnah externally and achieving fana’ from the nafs and baqa’ in Allah internally, plus having ma’rifah of Allah’s Names and Attributes, adab with Allah, and good character with all of Allah’s creatures. That is the deen.” [Qanabil, “Is the Child of the Sheikh Like the Sheikh,” 17] Having a Good Opinion I remind you of Allah, the Blessed and Exalted, and with praise for Him. This is something one should never do without. The continuous praise of Allah, the Blessed and Exalted, is necessary for anyone who wishes to maintain the blessings of Allah. If you praise Him, He will increase His blessings upon you. Do not become accustomed to the blessings to the point where you consider them a natural occurrence and forget that they are gifts from Allah, the Blessed and Exalted. Blessings are not restricted unless you stop praising. Praise Allah continually, in every movement and in every stillness, in every blessing. Not a single minute goes by without Allah granting you numerous blessings during that minute alone. There is nothing that increases blessings except through the praise of Allah, the Blessed and Exalted. This Ummah (community) is secreted in praise alone, for within it, there is Muhammad and within it, there is Ahmad. Their last supplication was ‘All praise is due to Allah, the Lord of all the worlds.’ We are secreted in this world and in the hereafter with praise to Allah, the Blessed and Exalted. The gates to Allah, the Blessed and Exalted, are opened through praise alone. Praise Allah in all its forms, secretly and openly, in words and in deeds, immediately and consistently, in the heart and in actions. Remember Allah often, and do not forget Him in your hearts. Forgetting Allah in your heart means that you have blocked the door against yourself. Remembering Allah opens the door to goodness, and forgetting the self closes the door to evil. The self is always the source of trouble for people. The only thing that prevents people from unity and harmony is the self. Remember Allah within yourselves so that you may progress with Allah, the Blessed and Exalted, bringing you good and keeping evils away. The true believer loves for his brother what he loves for himself. One cannot perfect his faith unless he loves for his brother what he loves for himself. The ones who are truly knowledgeable are those whom Allah, the Blessed and Exalted, loves more than they love themselves, for He loves them more than they love themselves. And this applies to every person. It is fitting for anyone who knows anyone that Allah’s love for him is stronger than his love for himself. So, his love for that person should be stronger than his love for himself. Souls always come up with reasons, like self-admiration, good assumptions about themselves, belittling your brother, and having bad thoughts about him. These reasons are destructive. If someone admires himself, he is ruined, and if someone belittles his brother, he is ruined. A believer should not look at another believer except believing that he is better than himself. How can you believe that he is better than yourself? It is that you believe that Allah saved him because of your good thoughts about yourself, and He gave him good thoughts about your brother. I will mention to you the description by which someone can have better thoughts about his brother than about himself. When a person is poor and sees his brother as wealthy, he should have good thoughts about his brother and say, ‘Allah has given him wealth, and as for me, I don’t have the means to spend for the sake of Allah.’ So, if he becomes better than me, it’s because Allah has obliged me to spend, and I am not able to do so. If I were rich and my brother poor, I should say that my brother is better than me because Allah has obliged me to spend, and he hasn’t. If my brother is ignorant and I am knowledgeable, I should have good thoughts about my brother and say, ‘Allah has not given him knowledge, but He has obliged me to act upon knowledge that He has not obliged upon him. So, I have done what Allah has obliged upon me.’ Therefore, having good thoughts about your brother and having bad thoughts about yourself are based on this. If Allah gives someone good thoughts about his brother and bad thoughts about himself, He has opened the door to goodness for him and closed the door to evil. When a group of people reflects, they may not know among themselves who is the greater and who is the lesser. The greater is the one who is great in the sight of Allah, and the lesser is the one who is small in the sight of Allah. It is possible for a person to be small but great in the sight of Allah, and all his actions are flawed. Another may be flawed but his actions are those of the small. Thus, the heart is what determines a person. If it bears good fruit, a small person can become great, and if it fails to bear fruit, a great person can become small. We do not know who among us is greater or smaller; only Allah, the Blessed and Exalted, knows this. Each one of us should think well of his brother, believing that he is the greater. If one of us is great and sees his brother as small, he should think well of him, for we do not see what is in his heart. If a person is great, outward appearances may suggest that, but as for good assumptions, we should not miss out on them and believe that he is better than us. Therefore, the reason for division among us is only bad assumptions. Beware of bad assumptions because it is the door to all evil, the door to denial, and the door to division, and it originates from the self. Good assumptions from Allah are certainly good, and bad assumptions are certainly bad. There is no good except from Allah, and there is no evil except from the self. Be united in His religion. This worldly life is a fleeting enjoyment, the real return is to Allah, the Blessed and Exalted, on the Day of Judgment. Take heed and flee from what distances you from Allah, and strive to compete in getting closer to Him. Do not mingle nor keep company with any people other than those who bring you close to Him. As we read in the Hikam of Ibn Ata’illah: “Do not accompany someone whose condition does not enliven you and whose words do not direct you to Allah.” And exhort one another to truth and exhort one another to patience, and let not the life of this world deceive you, nor let the Deceiver deceive you about Allah. May you always be blessed with all goodness, well-being, happiness, and mutual love for Allah’s sake. Salaam, Muhammad al-Mishri Competing &amp; Visiting for Allah | Shaykh Muhammad Mishry Then strive and know that striving is only today. Today is action without reckoning. Tomorrow is reckoning without action. None of us knows how much is left of their life. What remains of life is that in which we will collect the provisions that will be with us on the Day of Resurrection. The Day of Resurrection is a day without end. Dunya is evanescent. The return is to Allah Blessed and Exalted in the Akhirah, which is the Abode of Permanence, so let us all prepare for that abode with what has brought us from our land, country, wealth and family to approach Allah Blessed and Exalted, to make provisions for our Akhirah. Those who have come here only to reach Madinah (Medina Baye, Kaolack) have reached their goal. Those who have come only to reach the Shaykh have reached their goal. However, the truth - which is indeed the truth – is that it is not the form of Madinah that has brought us here and the Shaykh’s form, also, is not what has brought us here. Only approaching Allah Blessed and Exalted has brought us here, and standing at the doors of His grace (fadl). Befriend Allah’s Essence - Blessed and Exalted. In the forms, we are united. The group, Alhamdulillah, is one, everyone with their brother and beloved. However the truth – which is indeed the truth – is that no two of us are in the same place. Each of us contemplates Allah (Blessed and Exalted) in the place where they are, each of us befriends Allah (Blessed and Exalted), and each of us contemplates Allah according to their faith and ma ʽ rifah (existential familiarity), so no two of us are in the same place. Compete in what has brought you here. Let none of you say: “I miss my brother. He is not with me in this land.” What has brought you here is not your brother. What brought you here is the contemplation of Allah Blessed and Exalted and proximity to Him. The Wali Kills Your Nafs “...the wali’s first step in wilayah is to kill their nafs and then fight other nufus. This means that whenever they find an op- portunity to kill someone’s nafs, they will kill it with Allah, as in Allah erases whatever He wills from the nafs’ sense of self and establishes whatever He wills of His own sense of self. It is no secret that the people of nufus are regimented soldiers who know their enemies and are constantly striving against anyone who is not on their side, waging war against whoever wages war against their nufus and fighting against whoever fights them. They are thus enemies of the Truth, while the wali is the enemy of falsehood.” Shaikh Muhammad al-Mishry On the Khalifa of Allah "The objective and goal of the existence of the Khalifa of Allah is Gnosis [ma'rifa] of Allah, because Allah does not die in order to leave behind a Khalifa to succeed Him, and He is not incapacitated in order for him to have a Khalifa to help Him and support Him. Therefore what is the secret behind the existence of the Khalifa? The answer to that is that Allah was with no direction attributable to Him, and where eyes cannot perceive Him, -&gt;&gt; so He brought to us a Khalifa who has a direction, and who can be perceived by the vision of the eyes.*&lt;&lt;- For the existence of the Khalifa is for our benefit and not due to any need of Allah Ta'ala for him, because Allah is independent from needing this world before the world, and along with the world and after the world."
